    • Project Chance – Introduction
    The goals for the participating students are: 1- to enhance their ability to become independent learners. 2- to develop confidence in themselves as learners. 3- to improve literacy and numeracy skills. 4- to participate productively in academically heterogeneous classrooms. 5- to experience a variety of instructional strategies. 6- to deepen understanding of subject matter content. 7- to enhance awareness of potential contributions to a learning community.   • Project-Chance-Kompan-Games-Part-One
    • Project C.H.A.N.C.E. – Kompan Games Part One Children's Health, Achievement, Nutrition, and Community Empowerment. Project Chance students, along with Mr. Pritchett, invented a new game for the playground and the internet called “Kompan”. Based on the old lumberjack sport of “Logrolling” – this new playground game involves two contestants, each stand on a large wheel that spins. One or the other starts “walking” (or “side stepping”) on the wheel and the other is forced to keep up or ride it out. The contest involves attempting to stay on the wheel while trying to cause the competitor to lose their balance and fall into the mulch. This game is only played with adult supervision. Directed by Harold B. Pritchett. www.projectchance.00server.com

  • Character Education that works – Anger Management Song
    • Character Education that works – Anger Management Song Children (K-5) learn how to control their anger by making better choices when they are upset and about to fight in school. Choices are given in the words to the song and the class talks about what action can be taken to avoid a brawl in school, at home, or on the streets. The class sings the song along with music and afterwards they discuss the meaning. After children learned the song, the words are used to help them when they are facing internal drama. Example – Did you count to ten? – Did you walk away? - How can you control your anger the next time? Directed by Harold B. Pritchett, Character Education Teacher
